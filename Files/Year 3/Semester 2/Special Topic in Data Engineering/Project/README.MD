# 🏭 Special Topic in Data Engineering — Project (PPG Industry)

**Year 3 · Semester 2 · Special Topic in Data Engineering**

An industry-linked data engineering project completed in collaboration with PPG — applying cloud platforms, distributed processing, and analytical pipeline techniques to a real-world industrial problem.

---

## 🎯 Overview

The PPG Industry Project was the major deliverable of Special Topic in Data Engineering. It required applying the data engineering concepts and tools covered across the semester — Azure, Spark, DuckDB, dbt, and AI evaluation — to address a real business problem provided by PPG.

---

## 🛠️ Tools and Technologies

- Microsoft Azure (cloud data storage)
- Apache Spark / PySpark (distributed processing)
- DuckDB, dbt (analytical pipeline)
- Python (data engineering scripts)
- PDF report and presentation slides

## 📖 What I Did

I contributed to the PPG industry project as part of a team — working through the full data engineering pipeline from data ingestion and preparation through transformation, analysis, and output. The work involved applying Azure storage, Spark processing, and analytical pipeline tools to the industry dataset and problem. I also contributed to writing the case study report and preparing the presentation slides that document the approach, implementation, and findings.

---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** The experience of applying data engineering skills to a real industry problem with a real stakeholder. Industry projects introduce constraints — data quality issues, ambiguous requirements, limited access — that academic exercises do not. Navigating those constraints while producing a useful output developed both technical and professional skills.

**Suggestions for improvement:** More structured feedback from the industry partner during the project — not just at the final presentation — would help teams course-correct earlier and produce outputs that better match industry expectations.

**Other thoughts:** The PPG project was the most meaningful technical work of the degree. Working on a problem that matters to a real organisation, under real constraints, and presenting the findings professionally confirmed that the data engineering skills I have built across three years are applicable to genuine industry challenges. It is the experience I will point to most readily when describing what my degree has prepared me for.
