---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** The ability to write about technical topics in a formal, structured, and evidence-supported way. Translating practical technical knowledge into academic language requires a different kind of precision — choosing words carefully, supporting claims with sources, and organising ideas into a coherent argument rather than a sequence of facts.

**Suggestions for improvement:** A workshop on how to read and synthesise academic papers efficiently would be valuable before the article tutorial. Understanding how to extract relevant information from dense research papers and incorporate it into a well-cited argument is a skill that takes practice.

**Other thoughts:** Technical writing is often undervalued in engineering education, but it matters enormously in professional practice. Reports, proposals, documentation, and articles are how technical work is communicated, evaluated, and preserved. This tutorial gave me a structured opportunity to develop that skill in a data engineering context.
