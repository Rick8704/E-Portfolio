## 📖 What I Learned

Business Intelligence covered the practical pipeline from raw data to business-facing insight. I learned to use Alteryx to clean, transform, and prepare datasets for analysis; build analytical models that answer business questions; and design Power BI dashboards that communicate findings clearly to non-technical audiences.

The three-phase project structure — preparation, modelling, presentation — mirrors a real-world BI workflow, requiring both technical proficiency and the ability to communicate results effectively.

---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** A complete BI workflow from data ingestion to stakeholder presentation. Understanding how Alteryx and Power BI fit together in an end-to-end pipeline, and how to design a dashboard that communicates clearly without overwhelming the viewer, has been directly useful in data engineering work.

**Suggestions for improvement:** Including more exposure to SQL-based BI tools alongside Alteryx — such as dbt for data transformation — would provide a broader perspective on the BI tooling landscape and prepare students for environments where GUI-based tools are not used.

**Other thoughts:** The most important lesson from BI was that the quality of the insight depends as much on how it is presented as on how it is produced. A technically correct analysis that is poorly communicated does not help decision-makers. Designing dashboards forced me to think from the reader's perspective — which is a skill that applies across all output-facing data engineering work.
