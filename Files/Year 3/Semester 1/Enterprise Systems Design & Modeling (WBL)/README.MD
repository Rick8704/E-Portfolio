## 📖 What I Learned

Enterprise Systems Design & Modeling covered the full process of designing enterprise-scale information systems — from requirements gathering and business process analysis to UML modelling, architecture design, and system specification. The WBL component connected these methods to real-world enterprise contexts.

The PPG industry assignment introduced industry collaboration — applying enterprise systems thinking to a professional context. The hotel management project required designing a complete enterprise system, and the SAP case study examined how large-scale commercial systems are structured and deployed.

---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** An understanding of how enterprise-scale systems are designed, documented, and managed — and how different stakeholders (business, technical, and user) have different requirements that the design must accommodate simultaneously.

**Suggestions for improvement:** More exposure to actual enterprise system demonstrations — seeing how SAP, Oracle, or similar platforms work in practice — would make the design principles more grounded. Case studies are more effective when they can be connected to observable systems.

**Other thoughts:** ESDM was the course that most closely simulated professional enterprise systems work. Managing the complexity of a large-scale design, communicating it through formal models, and connecting it to business requirements is challenging — but directly relevant to the kinds of systems that data engineers build and maintain.
