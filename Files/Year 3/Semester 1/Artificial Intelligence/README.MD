## 📖 What I Learned

Artificial Intelligence gave me a structured view of how machines can exhibit intelligent behaviour. I learned uninformed and informed search strategies (BFS, DFS, A*), knowledge representation using logic and semantic networks, probabilistic reasoning with Bayesian networks, and the fundamentals of machine learning — supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.

The group assignments applied AI concepts to real scenarios, requiring both technical understanding and written communication of results and reasoning.

---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** A conceptual foundation for understanding and evaluating AI systems. Knowing how search algorithms explore state spaces, how probabilities propagate through a Bayesian network, and how a neural network learns through gradient descent gives me a more grounded perspective on the AI tools I use in data engineering.

**Suggestions for improvement:** More implementation work — coding search algorithms, building simple classifiers, training a neural network from scratch — would deepen understanding beyond the conceptual level. Theory supported by implementation is far more durable.

**Other thoughts:** AI is a course that keeps paying dividends. Every time I encounter a machine learning tool, a recommendation system, or an AI-driven data pipeline, the concepts from this course give me a framework for understanding what is happening and why. The group assignments also strengthened my ability to explain technical AI concepts in clear written English.
