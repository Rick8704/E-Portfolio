## 📖 What I Learned

Artificial Intelligence introduced foundational AI concepts — search algorithms, knowledge representation, reasoning under uncertainty, and machine learning techniques. The course connected AI theory to practical applications in data-driven systems.

Database Programming went beyond basic SQL into stored procedures, triggers, views, transactions, and performance tuning. It deepened understanding of how databases support complex application logic and large-scale data operations.

Enterprise Systems Design & Modeling (WBL) covered the design of large-scale enterprise information systems. The WBL component required applying UML modelling, system architecture design, and requirements analysis to a real project — the ESDM Hostel Management System.

---

## 💭 Reflection

This semester marked a significant step towards professional-level work. The ESDM project was the most complex team undertaking I had been part of — requiring careful coordination, clear documentation, and iterative development across multiple system components.

Database Programming changed how I write queries and design schemas. Understanding performance implications — indexes, execution plans, and transaction management — made me more disciplined about how I work with data.

Artificial Intelligence opened a new perspective on how systems can make decisions. Seeing how search and learning algorithms work at a fundamental level gave me a clearer understanding of the AI tools I use in data engineering work.
