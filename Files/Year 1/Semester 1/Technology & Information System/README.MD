## 📖 What I Learned

Technology & Information System provided a broad view of how technology intersects with organisational needs. The Design Thinking framework — empathise, define, ideate, prototype, and test — gave me a structured approach to problem-solving that goes beyond writing code.

The group assignment required working through the full Design Thinking process as a team, identifying a real problem, generating ideas, and producing a prototype concept. This was my first experience of collaborative, user-centred design in an academic setting.

---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** A user-centred perspective on technology. Understanding that systems are built for people — and that the best solutions come from understanding user needs before writing a single line of code — has shaped how I approach every project since.

**Suggestions for improvement:** Exposure to real organisations or user groups for the empathise phase would have made the Design Thinking process more realistic and valuable. Working from assumptions rather than genuine user feedback limited the quality of insights.

**Other thoughts:** This course introduced skills that technical courses often overlook — communication, collaboration, and empathy. These have proven just as important as technical ability in team projects and industry-facing work.
