## 📖 What I Learned

The first semester provided the groundwork for all future computing study. Digital Logic introduced binary systems, logic gates, and combinational circuits that underpin every digital device. Discrete Structure covered set theory, logic proofs, graph theory, and combinatorics — the mathematical language of computer science.

Programming Technique I offered the first experience of structured programming in C++, covering variables, control flow, functions, and arrays. Technology & Information System gave a broader perspective on how information systems are designed, used, and managed in organisations.

---

## 💭 Reflection

Starting university was both exciting and challenging. The volume and pace of new concepts — especially in Discrete Structure and Digital Logic — required a different approach to learning than school. I had to build the habit of reading ahead, working through problems independently, and seeking help when stuck.

Programming Technique I was the course that sparked the most curiosity. Writing my first programs in C++ and seeing them produce output was a rewarding experience. It gave me a clear reason to engage with the more theoretical content in other courses.

This semester taught me that every layer of computing — from logic gates to information systems — is connected, and that understanding the fundamentals makes everything else easier to learn.
