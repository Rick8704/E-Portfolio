# 📘 Discrete Structure

**Year 1 · Semester 1**

This course covered the mathematical foundations of computer science — set theory, logic, proof techniques, relations, functions, graph theory, and combinatorics.

---

## 📂 Work

No assignment or lab subfolders recorded for this course.

---

## 📖 What I Learned

Discrete Structure introduced the formal language of mathematics that underpins computer science. I learned to write logical proofs, work with sets and relations, reason about functions, and analyse graphs and trees. Combinatorics gave me tools for counting and probability that directly support data analysis.

The course required a different kind of thinking from programming — more abstract and formal — but the skills it built have quietly supported every technical course since.

---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** A rigorous way of reasoning about problems. The ability to construct a logical argument, verify it step by step, and identify where an assumption fails has made me a more careful thinker in both code and design.

**Suggestions for improvement:** More connections to practical computing examples — such as showing how graph algorithms relate to the graph theory content — would have helped motivate the abstract material.

**Other thoughts:** Discrete Structure was one of the hardest courses in Year 1, but in hindsight it prepared me well for algorithms, database theory, and AI. The investment was worth it.
