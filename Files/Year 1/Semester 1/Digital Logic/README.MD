**Year 1 · Semester 1**

This course introduced the fundamental building blocks of digital systems — binary number systems, Boolean algebra, logic gates, and combinational circuit design.

## 📖 What I Learned

Digital Logic gave me my first look at how computers work at the hardware level. I learned to represent numbers in binary and hexadecimal, apply Boolean algebra to simplify logic expressions, and design combinational circuits using logic gates. The course showed that even complex digital behaviour can be broken down into simple logical operations.

The project required me to apply circuit design principles to a real-world scenario — a lift control system — which made the theory feel tangible and purposeful.

---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** A foundational understanding of how digital hardware operates. Knowing how logic gates combine to form circuits helped me understand the lower layers of computing that I had previously taken for granted.

**Suggestions for improvement:** More hands-on simulation exercises throughout the semester would have reinforced the theoretical content. Practical design tasks like the project were the most effective learning experiences.

**Other thoughts:** This course taught me to think systematically. Breaking a problem into smaller logic components and verifying each part before combining them is a habit I have carried into programming and data engineering work.
