# 📘 System Analysis & Design (WBL)

**Year 1 · Semester 2**

This work-based learning course introduced structured approaches to analysing business requirements and designing information systems — covering requirements gathering, data flow diagrams, entity-relationship modelling, and system documentation.

---

## 📂 Work

No assignment or lab subfolders recorded for this course.

---

## 📖 What I Learned

System Analysis & Design taught me to approach a system from the user and business perspective before writing any code. I learned to gather and document requirements, draw data flow diagrams (DFDs) and entity-relationship diagrams (ERDs), produce system specifications, and plan a development process.

The WBL component connected these methods to realistic scenarios, requiring the application of analysis and design techniques to problems with real-world constraints.

---

## 💭 Reflection

**What I gained:** A structured way of thinking about systems before building them. The discipline of documenting requirements and designing a solution on paper before implementation has saved significant time in subsequent projects.

**Suggestions for improvement:** Working with an actual client or organisation — even a simulated one — for the WBL component would make the requirements gathering exercise more realistic. Real clients change their minds, have unclear needs, and prioritise differently than a worksheet expects.

**Other thoughts:** System Analysis & Design is easy to underestimate, but its influence is felt in every project. Rushing into implementation without a clear design leads to rework, scope creep, and integration problems. This course taught me to slow down at the beginning so that the rest of the work goes more smoothly.
